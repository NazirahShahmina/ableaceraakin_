Shariah-compliant commodity trading refers to conducting transactions in line with Islamic financial principles, which prohibit interest (riba), uncertainty (gharar), and the trading of prohibited (haram) goods. Ableace Raakin ensures that all our commodity trades meet these requirements, offering ethical and transparent solutions for our clients.

We work closely with Shariah advisors and certifying bodies to ensure that all of our transactions adhere to Islamic principles. We carefully vet suppliers and products, and ensure that every trade is free from interest, excessive uncertainty, and unethical practices.

Ableace Raakin operates globally, with a strong presence in Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Africa. We have a wide network of suppliers and buyers, allowing us to facilitate international trade efficiently and reliably.

To begin trading with us, you can reach out via our Contact Us page, where we will gather your requirements and guide you through the process. We will provide you with the necessary details and support to make your trade seamless and Shariah-compliant.

We prioritize sustainability and ethical sourcing by working only with suppliers who adhere to responsible environmental and social practices. We ensure that all commodities are sourced in line with our commitment to ethical and Shariah-compliant trade.

Murabahah is a type of Islamic financing where the seller discloses the cost and profit margin to the buyer. Unlike conventional loans, Murabahah is a sale agreement where the buyer purchases goods at an agreed profit margin — compliant with Islamic law as it involves no interest.

In a Murabahah transaction, the seller bears the risk of ownership until the asset is sold to the buyer. Once the buyer purchases the asset, the risk is transferred to the buyer.
